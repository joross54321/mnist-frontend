How Preprocessing Works
  1. Convert image to greyscale
  2. Auto-invert if background is bright
  3. Resize to 28×28 (Lanczos)
  4. Gaussian blur (radius 0.5)
  5. Normalise pixels to [0, 1]
  6. Flatten to 784-dim vector
Analysis
The baseline MLP [784→128→64→10] trained with Adam achieved >97% test accuracy, confirming that shallow MLPs suffice for MNIST. Experiment A (Dropout 0.5) reduced the train–validation gap, improving generalisation at a small accuracy cost. Experiment B demonstrated Adam's superiority over SGD within a fixed 10-epoch budget due to its adaptive learning rates. Experiment C's heavily reduced [784→16→10] architecture showed clear underfitting, validating that model capacity must match dataset complexity. Experiment D's deeper [784→128→64→32→10] network achieved only marginal improvement, suggesting MNIST does not require depth beyond two hidden layers.
